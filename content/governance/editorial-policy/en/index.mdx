# Editorial Policy

## Editorial Standards

All content on Psychpedia undergoes rigorous review to ensure accuracy, clinical relevance, and patient safety. Our editorial process follows these principles:

### Evidence-Based Content

- Content must be supported by peer-reviewed research, clinical guidelines, or established medical literature
- Sources are cited and evidence strength is clearly indicated
- We use a grading system (A/B/C/D) to indicate evidence quality

### Clinical Review

- All content is reviewed by qualified healthcare professionals
- Reviewers must have relevant clinical experience and credentials
- Content is reviewed for accuracy, completeness, and clinical utility

### Regular Updates

- Content is reviewed on a regular schedule (typically 6-12 months)
- Updates reflect new evidence, guideline changes, and clinical practice evolution
- Changelog entries document significant updates

### Patient Safety

- Patient safety is the primary consideration in all content
- Appropriate warnings, contraindications, and precautions are included
- Content emphasizes the importance of professional clinical judgment

## Review Process

1. **Initial Review**: Content is reviewed by a qualified clinician
2. **Evidence Check**: Sources and evidence strength are verified
3. **Safety Review**: Content is checked for patient safety considerations
4. **Final Approval**: Content is approved for publication

## Editorial Board

Our editorial board consists of qualified healthcare professionals with expertise in psychiatry, mental health, and clinical practice. Reviewers are selected based on their clinical experience, credentials, and commitment to evidence-based practice.

## Corrections

If you identify an error in our content, please report it using the "Report Issue" feature on each page. We take all reports seriously and will review and correct verified errors promptly.
