# Sources Policy

## Acceptable Evidence Sources

We prioritize evidence from the following sources, in order of preference:

### 1. Clinical Guidelines
- Guidelines from recognized professional organizations (e.g., APA, NICE, WHO)
- Evidence-based treatment guidelines
- Practice parameters from specialty societies
- Consensus statements from expert panels

### 2. Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses
- Cochrane reviews
- Published systematic reviews in peer-reviewed journals
- Meta-analyses of randomized controlled trials
- Network meta-analyses

### 3. Randomized Controlled Trials (RCTs)
- Published RCTs in peer-reviewed journals
- Large, well-designed trials
- Multi-center trials
- Industry-sponsored trials (with appropriate disclosure)

### 4. Drug Labels and Regulatory Information
- FDA-approved prescribing information
- EMA product information
- Regulatory agency safety communications
- Drug interaction databases (e.g., DrugCentral, Micromedex)

### 5. Observational Studies
- Large cohort studies
- Case-control studies
- Registry data
- Pharmacovigilance data

### 6. Expert Consensus
- Expert panel recommendations
- Clinical experience from recognized experts
- Case series and case reports (for rare conditions or events)

## Source Quality Criteria

We evaluate sources based on:

- **Methodology**: Study design, sample size, statistical methods
- **Reproducibility**: Can findings be replicated?
- **Relevance**: Applicability to clinical practice
- **Currency**: Recency of the evidence
- **Bias**: Potential conflicts of interest or study limitations

## Citation Standards

- All sources are cited in the references section
- Citations include author, title, journal, year, and DOI when available
- Links to sources are provided when publicly available
- Regulatory information includes version dates

## Limitations

We acknowledge that:
- Evidence evolves, and new research may change recommendations
- Some areas have limited high-quality evidence
- Clinical judgment is always required
- Our content is not a substitute for professional medical advice
